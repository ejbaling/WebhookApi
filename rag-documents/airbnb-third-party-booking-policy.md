# Airbnb Third-Party Booking Policy

## Policy Overview

Airbnb requires that the person staying must also be the one who makes the booking. Bookings made for someone else are considered third-party bookings and are not allowed under Airbnb’s Terms of Service.

This policy helps ensure that both the guest and the host are properly covered by Airbnb’s protections, verification, and insurance during the stay.

## Guest Options

To ensure the reservation is compliant and protected, the available options are:

- The booking guest can stay as originally reserved.
- If another person (for example, a spouse or family member) will stay instead, the current reservation should be cancelled and the actual guest should make a new booking using their own Airbnb account.

## Host Guidance

Following Airbnb’s booking policy helps ensure:

- Proper guest identity verification
- Protection under Airbnb support and safeguards
- Eligibility for Airbnb host protections and insurance coverage

These steps help protect both the guest and the host and ensure the reservation remains within Airbnb’s platform policies.
